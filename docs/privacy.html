Distill — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-27

Distill is a Chrome extension that helps you read long articles with AI-assisted summaries, comprehension check-ins, highlight analysis, and reading-time tools.

Distill has no backend of its own in the default configuration. It runs entirely in your browser using an AI API key that you provide. The developers of Distill do not operate a server that receives your data in this mode, and do not collect, store, sell, or share your personal information.

  • Your AI API key is stored only on your device (Chrome local storage). It is never transmitted to us.
  • When you ask for a summary/explanation/analysis, the relevant article text is sent directly from your browser to your chosen AI provider (Google Gemini or Anthropic), using your key. It does not pass through any Distill server.
  • No analytics, no ad trackers, no telemetry.
  • You can remove your key and all local data at any time.

What data Distill handles

Your AI API key — stored in chrome.storage.local on your computer only; used to authenticate requests you initiate to your AI provider; never sent to the Distill developers.

Article and page content — when you trigger an AI action, the relevant text from the page is sent over HTTPS directly to your selected AI provider. Distill keeps no server-side copy. Recent summaries may be cached locally so you can resume reading.

Reading state and preferences — progress, per-site preferences, accent color, etc. are stored locally and never leave your device.

Third-party AI providers

Depending on the provider you choose, your text is sent to:

  • Google Gemini — see the Gemini API terms. Content submitted on Google's free tier may be used to improve Google's products. For sensitive material, use a paid key or another provider/plan.
  • Anthropic Claude — see Anthropic's privacy policy. Anthropic does not train on API inputs/outputs by default.

You send data to these providers under your own account and key; their terms govern that relationship.

Optional self-hosted backend (advanced, off by default)

Distill includes an optional advanced mode that routes requests through a self-hosted or shared backend instead of your own key. It is disabled by default and the published extension designates no developer-operated default server for end users. If you enable it, the privacy terms of whoever runs that server apply.

What we do not do

We do not sell or rent your data, use it for advertising, embed third-party analytics, or collect your browsing history.

Removing your data

Remove your key in Settings → AI key → "Remove", or remove the extension to clear all local data.

Children

Distill is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them.
